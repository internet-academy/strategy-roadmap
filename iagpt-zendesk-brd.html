2. Executive Summary

IA is building IAGPT as a staged AI support platform. Phase 1 ingested Confluence documentation into an internal chatbot. Phase 2 addresses the Zendesk dependency by ingesting approved Zendesk knowledge into staff-only IAGPT and by defining the workflow foundation needed to eventually reduce or replace Zendesk.

Phase 2 does not approve immediate Zendesk cancellation or customer-facing AI. It supports a target readiness decision in February 2027, after Phase 4 live-server release, to decide whether Zendesk can be reduced or cancelled based on quality, workflow, contract timing, cost/savings, staff adoption, and support continuity.

Concrete numbers at a glance:

  • 58 Zendesk articles to ingest into IAGPT (approved knowledge only)
  • 3–4 support staff affected — they currently switch between Zendesk, Confluence, and internal docs to find answers
  • Estimated Zendesk cost: ~¥330,000–400,000/month (Suite Team, 4 agents at $55/agent/month) → ~¥4–4.8M/year
  • Staff time savings: ~2h/week per staff × 4 staff × ¥20,000/h × 52 weeks = ~¥8.3M/year
  • Total annual benefit if Zendesk replaced: ~¥12.3–13.1M/year (cost + time savings)
  • Ingestion effort: 58 articles × ~1.5h each = ~87h = ~¥1.74M one-time
  • Investment payback: ~2 months (staff time savings alone)

6. Business Problem and Opportunity

Current situation

Zendesk currently holds 58 approved support knowledge articles and operational workflows. This creates:

  1. Vendor dependency — IA does not control the knowledge platform. If Zendesk raises prices or changes features, IA has no leverage.
  2. Recurring contract cost — estimated ¥4–4.8M/year (Suite Team, 4 agents). This cost continues every year regardless of usage.
  3. Knowledge fragmentation — support knowledge is split across Zendesk, Confluence, and internal docs. Staff search 3 separate systems to find answers, wasting an estimated 2h/week per person.
  4. No AI integration path — Zendesk knowledge cannot be used in IAGPT AI workflows. Staff cannot get AI-assisted answers grounded in support knowledge.

The opportunity

Bring approved Zendesk knowledge into IAGPT first (Phase 2A), then use Phase 2 findings to identify which Zendesk functions can be replaced, which must be retained, and what must be built before a safe contract reduction or cancellation decision (Phase 2B).

Concrete benefit if Zendesk is fully replaced:

Benefit categoryAnnual savingsHow
Zendesk subscription cost¥4–4.8M/yearNo more per-agent subscription
Support staff time (4 staff × 2h/week)¥8.3M/yearOne unified search instead of 3 systems
Total annual benefit~¥12.3–13.1M/year
Note: Full replacement requires Phase 4 (customer-facing AI) to be live and accepted. Phase 2 builds the foundation — it does not guarantee cancellation.

7. Phase 2 Approach

Phase 2 has two tracks:

Phase 2A — Knowledge Ingestion Track

  • 58 approved Zendesk articles are imported into staff-only IAGPT
  • Support / School Desk reviews each article for accuracy and approval
  • Staff can search and use IAGPT for support answers
  • Estimated effort: ~87h (review + ingest + test)

Phase 2B — Replacement Readiness Track

  • Chat, ticketing, support history, escalation, reporting, and operational workflow needs are identified
  • Each Zendesk function is categorized: "replace with IAGPT" or "retain in Zendesk"
  • Gap analysis produced — what must be built before Zendesk can be cancelled
  • No replacement work is done in Phase 2 — only inventory and planning
